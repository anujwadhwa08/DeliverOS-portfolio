The Product Challenge

Food delivery coordination is a real-time multi-party problem: customer, restaurant, and driver must each take action at the right moment, in the right sequence, or the order fails. Existing solutions solve this with call centres, manual dispatch, and rigid scripted chatbots.

DeliverOS replaces all three with purpose-built AI agents that communicate in natural language, share a single order state machine, and handle every failure mode — from restaurant closures to driver no-shows — with defined automated recovery paths.

PM Insight
Designing with AI as a product capability, not a feature

Every AI decision in DeliverOS solved a specific user problem. Semantic search exists because customers don't browse like a database. The safety check exists because users can't tell when an AI is making something up. Three separate agents exist because a single generic AI gives worse answers to everyone. The MCP endpoint exists because agentic commerce is real — a customer's AI assistant should be able to order dinner the same way a human taps a button.

The hardest product work was designing the failure paths and the cross-channel identity layer. When a restaurant doesn't respond, when a driver goes offline mid-delivery, when a customer registered on Telegram tries to pay via MCP — every one is a defined state with a documented resolution. That's the difference between a demo and a platform.
