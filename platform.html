Zero-Friction Menu Setup
🌐
Method 1 — URL Import from any website

Paste any restaurant website URL. The platform loads the page, extracts every menu item, price, description, and category — and creates the full menu automatically. Works on any public restaurant site.

📸
Method 2 — Photo Photograph a physical menu

Send a photo of a printed menu in Telegram. AI reads the image and creates every item — name, price, category — in seconds. Menu photos and delivery photos are stored on Cloudflare R2.

📊
Method 3 — Spreadsheet Upload an Excel template

Download the platform's Excel template, fill it in, and upload it. AI validates every row, flags errors with line numbers, and imports only the clean items. Bulk updates work the same way.

Five AI Decisions That Define the Product
🔍
Decision 1 — Search Search by meaning, not keywords

Instead of dropdown filters and category taps, customers type what they feel like eating. The platform converts the query into a meaning-based search — matching restaurants and dishes by concept, not exact text. "Something light and healthy" finds the right results even if no menu says those exact words. No keyword matching required.

🤝
Decision 2 — Agents Three focused AI assistants, not one

Rather than one AI that tries to serve everyone, the platform gives each user type their own AI assistant — tuned to their context, their language, and their workflow. The customer's assistant knows about ordering and delivery. The restaurant's knows about orders and menus. The driver's knows about logistics and earnings. Each stays in its lane.

⚖️
Decision 3 — Trust Every AI response is fact-checked before you see it

Before any AI reply reaches a user, a second AI model checks it. It has one job: confirm that everything in the response actually exists — the restaurant name, the price, the delivery time. If the AI invents anything, the response is blocked. This runs on every message, from every assistant, every time — without the user ever knowing it's there.

🔄
Decision 4 — Reliability Every order follows a defined path

Orders move through a formal sequence of states — from payment confirmed to restaurant notified to driver assigned to delivered. Each step triggers automatic actions: notifications sent, payments captured, audit entries written. Every failure scenario — restaurant going offline, driver declining, no response in 10 minutes — is handled automatically, not discovered manually.

🔗
Decision 5 — Ecosystem Any AI agent can use the platform

The platform exposes an MCP (Model Context Protocol) server — a standard interface that lets any external AI assistant browse restaurants, place orders, and track deliveries on behalf of a user. A voice assistant, a concierge agent, or a smart home device can all interact with DeliverOS without a custom integration. The platform becomes a composable capability inside the AI ecosystem, not just a standalone app — and the same ordering logic, safety checks, and payment flows apply regardless of which AI triggered the request.

Product Principles — How the Platform Was Designed
Principle How It Shapes the Product User Impact
Channel-agnostic design The AI logic is completely separate from the channel it runs on — app, chat, or Telegram are just transport layers. Adding a new channel (WhatsApp, voice) requires zero changes to the AI or the ordering logic.
AI grounded in live data The AI can only respond using information it retrieved in real time — it has no access to invented content. Customers can trust that every restaurant, price, and availability shown is accurate right now.
No undefined failures Every edge case — cancellation, dispute, timeout, offline restaurant — is a named, handled state. Customers always receive a response. No order goes silent. No money goes missing without recovery.
Payments fully automated Stripe Payment Intents handle auth and capture. Stripe Connect routes payouts to restaurants and drivers. Stripe Identity verifies driver and restaurant KYC. All triggered by order state changes — no manual process. Customers get automatic refunds. Drivers and restaurants get paid without chasing. No human needed in the payment loop.
Media in the cloud Menu photos, cover photos, and proof-of-delivery images are stored on Cloudflare R2 with pre-signed URLs. No binary data in the database. Photo uploads are instant. Images are served globally from Cloudflare's edge. Storage scales independently of the app.
